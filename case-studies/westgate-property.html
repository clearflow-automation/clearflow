The problem

Westgate Property Group manages 112 residential units across 9 buildings in Mississauga and Brampton for 34 private landlords. With 8 staff and no dedicated property management software, their entire operation ran inside a collection of Excel files that had been patched together over 7 years.

The centrepiece was a rent roll workbook with 112 rows, one per unit — tracking tenant names, lease start and end dates, monthly rent, damage deposits, last payment date, and outstanding balance. Every month, the property manager manually cross-referenced bank deposits against this sheet to confirm which tenants had paid. With 112 units paying across the 1st, 7th, and 15th, this reconciliation took the better part of two days.

From the reconciled rent roll, a second staff member then manually built individual owner statements — one per landlord, 34 in total — by copying figures into a Word template, formatting each one, converting to PDF, and emailing them out. This took another two days. Total month-end process: 4 days, every month, without exception.

The errors were significant. In one quarter, three owners received statements with incorrect management fee deductions because a formula in the master sheet had been accidentally overwritten by a staff member pasting unformatted data. Two owners called to complain. One threatened to move their portfolio. The owner, Sandra, was manually auditing every statement before it went out — a process she described as "checking someone else's homework at 11pm every month."

The solution

ClearFlow rebuilt the rent roll from scratch as a structured data model with three separated layers — a protected master unit register, a monthly payment input sheet with dropdown validation, and a reconciliation engine that auto-matched incoming bank CSV exports against expected payments.

01

A bank reconciliation script that imports the monthly bank CSV export, auto-matches deposits to units using amount and date logic, flags unmatched transactions in red, and produces a confirmed-paid list in one click — replacing two days of manual cross-referencing.

02

A VBA statement generator that reads the reconciled data, builds all 34 owner statements automatically — applying each owner's property list, management fee percentage, and GST calculation — and exports them as individually named PDFs ready to attach and send.

03

A live arrears dashboard — a summary tab that automatically flags every unit more than 5 days overdue, colour-coded by severity, with the running total outstanding. The first run surfaced $11,200 in arrears that had been lost in the manual process.

All formula cells are locked and password-protected. Staff enter data only through validated input forms — no direct cell editing, no overwrite risk.
